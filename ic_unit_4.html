2. FAN-IN : The fan-IN of the logic gate is the number of inputs that the logic gate can handle. For example , if four gate require one unit load for each input then its fan-In is four.

3. FAN-OUT : The fan-IN also known as the loading factor is the maximum number of logic inputs that can be driven by an ouput. For Example, if a logic gate has a fan-OUT of 8 , then it can drive 8 logic inputs directly.

4. POWER DISSIPATION : The power dissipation of a logic circuit is defined as the suppy power neede for the logic gate to opeerate with 50% duty cycle at the specified junction. The heat dissipated at the junction.

5. FIGURE OF MERIT : It is defined as the product of power dissipated (mW) and propogation delay (nS). or Simply : 1mw * 1ns = 1p Joule.

6. NOISE MARGIN : The circuit ability to tolerate noise signal is reffered as the noise immunity , a quantitative measure of which is called noise margin.
