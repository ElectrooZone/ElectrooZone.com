* LOGIC GATE CHARACTERISTICS :-


1. Speed of Operations :- The speed of operation of a digital circuit is specified in terms of the propogation delay time. The delay times are measured between the 50% voltage levels of input and output wave forms. The input and output waveform is shown below:



2. Propagation Delay :-The progation delay time of the logic gate is taken as the message of the two delay times:



3. FAN-IN :- The fan-IN of the logic gate is the number of inputs that the logic gate can handle. For example , if four gate require one unit load for each input then its fan-In is four.


4. FAN-OUT :- The fan-IN also known as the loading factor is the maximum number of logic inputs that can be driven by an ouput. For Example, if a logic gate has a fan-OUT of 8 , then it can drive 8 logic inputs directly.


5. Power Dissipation :- The power dissipation of a logic circuit is defined as the suppy power neede for the logic gate to opeerate with 50% duty cycle at the specified junction. The heat dissipated at the junction.


6. Figure of Merit :- It is defined as the product of power dissipated (mW) and propogation delay (nS). or Simply : 1mw * 1ns = 1p Joule.


7. Noise Margin :- The circuit ability to tolerate noise signal is reffered as the noise immunity , a quantitative measure of which is called noise margin.
